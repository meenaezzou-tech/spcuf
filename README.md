# SPCUF — Supporting Parents, Children, United Families

> *"Know Your Rights. Protect Your Family."*

## What Is SPCUF?

SPCUF is a free, AI-powered legal case management platform built 
specifically for Texas families navigating the Child Protective 
Services (CPS) / DFPS system.

Built by a Texas parent who lived through a CPS investigation — 
with no coding background — using AI-powered tools and determination.

**Live App**: https://reunify-now.preview.emergentagent.com/

---

## The Problem

Every year thousands of Texas families face CPS investigations 
without knowing their rights, missing critical deadlines, and 
unable to afford attorneys. Most families:

- Don't know they can refuse entry without a court order
- Don't know CPS has mandatory deadlines they must follow
- Don't know they can legally record interactions
- Can't afford an attorney
- Don't know where to turn

SPCUF closes that gap — completely free.

---

## Who It's For

- Parents under CPS investigation
- Parents whose children have been removed
- Parents navigating service plans and court hearings
- Relatives seeking placement of a family member's child
- Legal aid advocates and attorneys

---

## Features

- **Case Vault** — Full case management with timeline tracking
- **AI Legal Advisor** — Powered by Claude AI with real-time web search
- **Your CPS Journey** — Day-by-day guided walkthrough of a Texas CPS case
- **Know Your Rights Card** — Screenshot-ready reference card for first contact
- **Rights & Law Library** — 15 Texas CPS legal topics with statute citations
- **Document Center** — 9 fillable legal document templates
- **Deadline Tracker** — Auto-calculates all Texas Family Code statutory deadlines
- **Conversation Log** — Document every CPS interaction with recording reminders
- **Complaint Center** — Step-by-step escalation workflows
- **Resource Center** — Texas legal aid, crisis support, and family services

---

## Legal Foundation

Grounded in:
- Texas Family Code Chapters 261, 262, 263, 264
- DFPS CPS Policy & Procedures Handbook
- Texas Administrative Code Title 40, Part 19
- CAPTA, ASFA, FFPSA, ICWA federal law
- U.S. Constitution 4th and 14th Amendment parental rights
